Bulls And Cows

a c++ terminal game

Bulls and Cows is an old brain teaser style game. The objective of the game is to guess a series of numbers - or a word - using clues called 'bulls' and 'cows'. These clues tell a player either about the position, or the value of the individual characters of the players guess. Using this information, the player tries to deduce the hidden series of numbers or word to win the game.


This console application is built in C++ from the ground up. It simulates a game of Bulls and Cows, assuming that there will be two people playing - a host, and a player.

The application runs in a series of states. At a high level the application outputs instructions, processes input, and then outputs a response to that input.

Input taken from the user is run through a series of validation methods to check if the word or guess is an isogram, the correct length, all lower case, and if there are any numbers.


Given correct output, the program then compares the guess to the hidden word. The player will recieve hints in the form of bulls or cows, and they will be told if the input they are entering is invalid - entering an invalid guess does not take a turn away from the player.

The game continues until either the player answers correctly, or they run out of turns (which are dynamically selected based on word length to scale difficulty accordingly).
